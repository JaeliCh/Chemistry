Introduction

In this "Red Cabbage pH indicator Experiment" we used the red cabbage juice as a natural way to test whether
everyday liquisds are acid, neutral, or basic. Red cabbage contains a pigment called anthocyanin that
change color depending on how acidic or basic something is. Thanks to the anthocyanin, we can see the PH of
a substance just by observing how its color changes. The main goal of the experiment was to observe how the
colors changed and compare them to the pH level of each substance we tested.

Hypothesis:

We thought that by adding red cabbage juice to different substances, they would change color depending on the
pH level of each one. These color changes would help us easily identify whether each substance was acidic,
neutral, or basic, and give us a better understanding of how pH works.
